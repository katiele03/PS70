My Twist on Battleship

A two-player Battleship board with LED feedback and sensors! Instead of calling out coordinates to sink a ship, you can physically tap coordinates on your grid. This causes the board to light up red for ‚Äúhit‚Äù or white for ‚Äúmiss‚Äù with sound effects.

3D Design and Fabrication (mold/cast, 3D print, lasercut, etc.)

  • Laser cutting: create grid plates with engraved coordinates and frosted tiles for LEDs
  • 3D printing: 3D print ships and encasing for board grids

Electronics (Input and Output)

Inputs:
  • Ship detection:
    • Magnetic detection: embed magnets in ships and place Hall effect sensors under each tile
    • Conductive detection: embed conductive foil in ships so that capacitive pads under grid tiles can sense when a ship is there
  • Capacitive touch grid: detects when a cell is tapped so user can attack opponent
Outputs:
  • RGB LEDs placed under each tile
    • red = hit opponent's ship
    • white = miss opponent's ship
    • green = ship is on grid (on own player's board)
  • Sound
    • explosion/boom = hit opponent's ship
    • splash = miss opponent's ship

Microcontroller Programming

  • Will be used to detect when player taps a tile
  • Detects when a ship is present on a tile
  • Turn on and off LEDs
  • Play sound
