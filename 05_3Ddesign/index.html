Overview

For this week’s assignment, I created a Harvard ring, scanned a fidget toy, and updated my final project site with 2 feasible strategies for my final project as well as a 3D model of the project, a bill of materials, and a timeline.

Harvard Ring

For my ring, I first created sketches for each of the letters. I spaced the letters 5 mm from each other with duplicate letters being copied and pasted. Here is the sketch of the letters that spell out ‘Harvard’.

Then, I measured the diameter of my middle finger, created the base of the ring, and projected the letters onto the surface of the base of the ring. I then went to the 3D printer to print out my ring. Unfortunately, I entered the diameter as the radius causing my ring to be humongous on my finger. Here is a video of my 3D printed ring that is double the size of my finger.

I reprinted my ring with the correct dimensions and waited for the printer with red PLA to be free because I wanted the ring to be crimson. Here is the image of my 3D printed ring on my finger.

Photogrammetry Scan

Then, I continued onto the second part of the assignment to scan something using a photogrammetry. I originally tried to scan my watch. Unfortunately, the scanner was having difficulty picking up my watch. I assume this was because of how reflective my watch was. Here is a video of the scanner struggling to scan my watch band.

Because my watch was unable to be scanned, I decided to use a fidget toy in the lost and found bin in the lab. I was able to scan a great image of the figdet toy in color. Here are some videos of the scan I was able to get.
