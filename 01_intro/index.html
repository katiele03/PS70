PS70: Final Project — Dynamic Grip Strengthener

I chose this song because it represents the goal of my final project: improving grip strength in individuals whose adherence to physical therapy is low due to fluctuating pain day to day.

Project Overview

For my final project, I designed and built a dynamic grip strengthener that automatically adjusts resistance based on the user’s input. Because hand dysfunction is strongly correlated with reduced quality of life, maintaining grip strength is especially important for individuals with arthritis, Parkinson’s disease, or those recovering from hand surgery.

System Concept

The device estimates grip force using spring compression measured by an ultrasonic sensor. Using Hooke’s Law, the system converts displacement into force and dynamically adjusts spring preload using stepper motors and lead screws. This allows the device to personalize resistance based on each user’s maximum comfortable force (MCF).

Miniaturization and Integration

Over the course of the project, I significantly reduced the size of the system by shortening aluminum guide rods and lead screws, reprinting structural components, and integrating the ultrasonic sensor directly into the gripping plate. These changes resulted in a more compact, portable device without sacrificing functionality.

Control Logic and Closed-Loop Behavior

The system operates in two stages: calibration and exercise. During calibration, the user performs several grips and the system records the maximum comfortable force. During exercise, the stepper motors adjust spring preload to maintain resistance at a fixed percentage of the user’s MCF while the user performs repeated grips.
